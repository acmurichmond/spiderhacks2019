About SpiderHacks

Join us at University of Richmond on the MLK Weekend for a 30 hour hackathon!

The second annual SpiderHacks, hosted by University of Richmond's ACM and sponsored by Capital One, aims to foster a passion for innovation and collaboration in technology through workshops, brainstorming, and building unique products. Team of three to four individuals will work together to develop a project to fruition over the course of 30 hours.

F.A.Q

  • What's the point of a hackathon?

    Have you ever had an idea but just couldn't work on it due to resources, time, or just lack of motivation? Now's your chance to try making it! Major League Hacking describes a hackathon as an "invention marathon" if that gives you any idea.

  • What is SpiderHacks?

    SpiderHacks is a hackathon hosted by ACM at UR where you along with other developers, entrepreneurs, and designers work together to create a product. This product can be a web app, a mobile app, whatever!

  • When and where is the event?

    The event takes place from 10:30 am January 19th to 4:30pm January 20th. However, note that official events start at 10:30 am and you will have to register, so try to get to the venue sometime between 10 am and 10:30 am. The event is held at University of Richmond at Alice Haynes Commons Room, Tyler Haynes Commons Building, 270 Richmond Way.

  • Who can attend?

    Any current students can attend, but students from UR, VCU, and local high schools are especially encouraged to attend since they are in the area!

  • What if I'm not that good at programming or I don't know how to make websites/mobile apps?

    That's okay! Come to the hackathon to learn about these things and to try and create a product. Learning is part of the hackathon experience.

  • Why should I go?

    There will be free food, goodies, and prizes! You should also go if you've never been to a hackathon before to see if you like it. Hopefully, this will encourage you to go to bigger, better hackathons!

  • How big can the teams be? What if I don't have a team?

    The teams can be up to 4 people. If you don't have a team, that's okay, you can come alone and we'll do a team matching session at the beginning. You're also welcome to work on a product alone if you want to!

  • What if I don't finish?

    That's also okay! Since it's such a short hackathon, it'll be hard to completely flesh out a product. We want to recognize everyone's hard work, so if you have made some progress or have some features working, we really encourage you to present at product presentations. It's completely okay if you didn't finish.

  • Will there be travel reimbursements?

    Unfortunately, there will not be travel reimbursements since it is a local hackathon.

  • What should I bring?

    If you have a computer, bring that and the charger, and bring your phone.

  • Will food be provided?

    Yes! We will be providing lunch and dinner on Saturday, and dinner on Sunday! It will be completely free. If you have any dietary restrictions, be sure to let us know when you register.

  • Will there be hardware or other supplies?

    Unfortunately, no. Since it's a local hackathon, there will not be hardware or other things to build with provided. You should also bring your own laptop.
